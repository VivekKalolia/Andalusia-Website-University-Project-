Introduction:

What is the essential story being told by your site and what type of structure did you choose to implement?

I have created the Andalusia Training Centre website to foster engagement and learning through intuitive and enriching user experience. Our site is designed to connect learners, students, teachers, hobbyists and the community through a variety of programs, events and workshops.

The website is carefully made to attract a broad range of audiences. Our particular targets for the website are students, teachers and enthusiasts. The homepage serves as an entry point, providing an overview of Andalusia Training Centre's purpose, featuring the "About" section which gives a short summary of the history of the centre's opening, as well as the scope of the students we have managed to provide education to.

Visitors can delve deeper through dedicated pages, such as "Courses" Page which showcases the different range of courses we provide, as well as the "About" Page, which provides a brief overview, articulating the community's foundational objectives and mission. This page also features a Gallery, where visitors can look through different pictures of the organisation and its events.

At its core, Andalusia Training Centre mirrors the changing world of educational careers, creating a space where learning, teaching and community engagement come together.

Accessibility:

State 3 ways in which your site is accessible.

  1. Semantic HTML Structure: Throughout the website, I utilized semantic HTML elements to structure the content. This approach to semantic markup guarantees that the information is presented in a logical manner, enhancing its clarity for users and accessibility devices alike. Incorporating elements like <header>, <article>, <nav>, <main>, and <footer>, improves the site's navigability and understandability by establishing a coherent and significant structure for the content, thereby enhancing accessibility.
  2. Responsiveness: The Andalusia Training Centre's website is crafted to seamlessly be responsive and adjust across various screen dimensions and devices, ensuring a consistent user experience. By employing media queries, I have preserved the site's aesthetic appeal and functionality across different platforms. Additionally, the implementation of flexible layout strategies, including flexbox and percentage-based widths, further enhances the site's ability to adapt to any viewing environment.
  3. Image Accessibility Enhancement: To ensure the Andalusia Training Centre's website is accessible to everyone, including users with visual impairments, all images are equipped with descriptive alt attributes. This enables screen readers to effectively interpret and convey the purpose of each image, enriching the browsing experience for users who rely on assistive technology. Additionally, this also helps to show preview texts in instances where images fail to load on the website. This careful focus highlights my dedication to making the website welcoming for everyone and meeting web accessibility guidelines, making the website more navigable and comprehensible for a diverse audience.

Usability:

State 3 ways in which you considered the usability of your site.

  1. Clear Navigation: I have designed the Andalusia Training Centre's website navigation to be straightforward and user-friendly. The primary menu (navigation bar) is strategically positioned for easy visibility, and each menu item (or, nav-item), including Home, Courses, About, Events, and Contact, is clearly labeled to guide users effortlessly to the information they seek, whether they want to know more about the organisation or if they want to know the address of the organisation. For smaller screens, a mobile-responsive menu ensures that the site remains easy to use on any device. Lastly, I have introduced a feature that highlights the active page in the navigation bar by bolding the text (green bolded text, in my case). This visually indicates to users which section of the site they are currently viewing.
  2. Unified Design and Structure: The website’s usability is further enhanced by a uniform design and layout. A cohesive look is achieved through standardized typography, color schemes (in my case, mostly blue and green), and interactive elements like buttons and links. This consistency aids users in quickly recognizing navigational features, fostering a comfortable and confident user experience as they interact with the Andalusia Training Centre’s online presence.
  3. Dynamic Currency Conversion (On Cards - Courses Page): To cater to our local audience, the Andalusia Training Centre's website features an interactive and dynamic pricing button on the course cards (On "Courses" Page). With a simple hover over the price button, the cost is automatically converted from a widely recognized currency (USD) to Tanzanian Shillings, offering immediate and localized pricing for our local visitors. This function not only simplifies the decision-making process for potential local students by presenting costs in their native currency, but also retains the convenience for international users by initially displaying prices in USD, thus enhancing the site's global usability.

Learning:

State 3 things you had to learn or find out to create your site. How did you achieve that?

I have learnt a lot of concepts during the creation of the website. Some of the main lessons include:

  1. Customization of Bootstrap Framework: During the process of developing the Andalusia Training Centre's website, I dove deep into Bootstrap to ensure the site was both responsive and engaging. My learning path involved absorbing the best practices from YouTube tutorials as well as University lectures, enabling me to effectively utilize Bootstrap's responsive frameworks and components. However, understanding that a unique user experience is key, I have customized Bootstrap's templates, infusing my own design elements to go beyond the basics. This customization allowed me to balance the framework's robust structure with a personalized touch. This approach ensured the website achieved a balance between professional functionality and distinct visual identity, making the learning experience both enriching and essential for the project’s success.
  2. Advanced JavaScript for dynamics: In enhancing the user experience of the website, I learned about advanced JavaScript using resources like YouTube, to create dynamic scroll-reveal effects. This technique involves programming specific sections of the website to become visible only as the user scrolls down, adding an engaging layer of interactivity. By listening for scroll events on the window, I learned how to strategically apply an "active" class to elements marked with the ".reveal-scroll" class. This not only enriches the visual experience but also guides visitors' attention to important content at just the right moment. I also implemented an image slider, where the user can press buttons to navigate through various pictures. The process of integrating these effects deepened my understanding of JavaScript and its powerful capability to transform static web pages into lively, interactive experiences.
  3. CSS Box Model: Exploring the fundamentals of web design, I learned about the CSS box model, a crucial concept that defines how elements are laid out and interact within their boundaries on a webpage. This understanding was pivotal in crafting layouts that are both aesthetically pleasing and functionally robust. To deepen my grasp of this essential framework, I turned to the comprehensive documentation provided by MDN Web Docs - CSS Box Model (MDN Web Docs). The insights gained from these resources were instrumental in enabling me to precisely control padding, borders, margins, and the overall spacing around elements, ensuring that each component on the website was meticulously placed for optimal user experience. This foundational knowledge not only enhanced my design capabilities but also enriched my skill set, allowing for more intuitive and sophisticated website development.
